Values & Ethics
An assessment guide to assess the quality of care you are receiving from WRAPⓇ group facilitators, care providers and other supporters. If you find that these values and ethics are not being followed, you can take one or more of the following actions: ask the person or people to follow these values and ethics, or, in case of extreme violation to your state agency of protection and advocacy.

Click on a Value to see its indicator


It is understood that there is hope, that people can get well, stay well for long periods of time, and do the things they want to do with their lives.

Indicator: WRAPⓇ group facilitators, care providers and other supporters do not make dire predictions about your future. They have a hopeful attitude, talk with you about your recovery and encourage you to make hopeful plans for your future.

Self determination,personal responsibility,empowerment, and self-advocacy are stressed.

Indicator: You are encouraged to work on keeping control over your own life, or taking back that control if you don't have it. Others are not trying to take away your control over your own life.

You are encouraged to make your own decisions, or when applicable make decisions with the group, and personal sharing is encouraged.

Indicator: All decisions are made by the people who will be personally affected by them. In groups there is shared decision-making. Personal sharing within time and subject parameters is encouraged and supported.

You must be treated with dignity, compassion, mutual respect, and unconditional high regard at all times, and as equal to all others.

Indicator: You feel comfortable, safe and respected when you are in a WRAPⓇ group, with a care provider or other supporter.

You are accepted unconditionally as a unique and special person, including acceptance or diversity of culture, ethnicity, language, religion, race, gender, age, disability, sexual identity, and ability.

Indicator: You feel accepted by others and there are no signs of discrimination or discriminitory comments.

It is understood that there are "no limits" to recovery.

Indicator: Your goals and plans are supported without judgment.

You are always given the opportunity to explore choices and options, and are not expected to easily find simple, final answers.

Indicator: It is understood that you have personal choice and a variety of options to choose from.

All participation is voluntary.

Indicator: You are not forced to do anything you don't want to do.

It is understood that you are the expert on yourself.

Indicator: You are supported in speaking from your own experience and making your own decisions based on what you know about yourself.

The focus is on individual strengths, and away from perceived deficits.

Indicator: Your strengths are honored and valued, and others are not critical of you, there is no negative judgments and no deficit-based assessments.

Clinical, medical, and diagnostic language is avoided.

Indicator: Others use language that is easily understood.

It is understood that peers working together and learning from each other to increase mutual understanding, knowledge, and wellness promotion recovery.

Indicator: opportunities for peer support are available, easily accessible and recommended.

Recommended strategies are simple and safe for anyone. Strategies that may have harmful effects, or are potentially dangerous are not suggested or recommended.

Indicator: The focus is on strategies that are simple and safe, and away from strategies that might have harmful side effects. You are directed to non-biased resources that will help inform your decisions on these issues.

Difficult feelings and behaviors are seen as normal responses to traumatic circumstances and in the context of what is happening, and not as "symptoms" or as confirmation of a diagnosis

Indicator: Others respond to your difficult feelings and behaviors with validaton, compassion and support.
